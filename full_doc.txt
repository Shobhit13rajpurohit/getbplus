What Is GetB+?
GetB+ is a cross-border fintech platform that lets international travelers pay seamlessly across India using their existing Visa, Mastercard, or Amex card at any UPI QR-accepting merchant — with zero local bank account, zero wallet top-up, and zero cash exchange required.

India has the world's largest QR-payment economy, with over 131 billion UPI transactions processed in FY2024. Yet every one of the 11.5 million international tourists who arrive annually is locked out of this system — their global cards simply do not work at UPI QR codes, which dominate Indian merchant acceptance from street vendors to fine-dining restaurants.

GetB+ solves this at the infrastructure level: it tokenizes a foreign card, provisions a virtual UPI ID linked to that card via a licensed banking partner, and enables scan-and-pay at any of India's 350+ million UPI-enabled merchants — instantly, securely, and without the user needing to understand the underlying complexity.

Four-Line Product Elevator Pitch
GetB+ bridges the gap between global payment cards and India's QR economy. Travelers link their existing Visa, Mastercard, or Amex card, receive a virtual UPI identity, and pay anywhere in India by simply scanning a QR code. No Indian bank account. No currency exchange. No cash. Just tap, scan, and pay — powered by NPCI-grade infrastructure.

Website Page Overview

Page 1 — Home Page (getbplus.com)
  PURPOSE
The Home page has one job: convert a curious visitor into an app download within 60 seconds. It must answer three unconscious questions: "What is this?", "Why should I trust it?", and "What do I do next?" — in that order, before the user scrolls past the fold.

  AUDIENCE
International tourists pre-trip planning (Google search), NRIs researching before India visits, and direct word-of-mouth arrivals. Assume they are on mobile, not desktop.

Sections Inside the Home Page:
HERO: Bold headline + one-line sub + two CTAs (Download App / How It Works)
TRUST BAR: Card network logos (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) + "RBI-Compliant" + "NPCI Partner" + user count stat
PROBLEM STATEMENT: "India runs on QR. Your card doesn't." — visual split showing frustrated tourist vs. smooth GetB+ user
HOW IT WORKS: 3-step horizontal flow with icons + micro-copy (Link Card → Get UPI ID → Scan & Pay)
FEATURES GRID: 6 core features in card layout
STATS SECTION: 350M+ merchants · 11.5M tourists/year · <3 min setup · 99.5% uptime
SOCIAL PROOF: 3 traveler testimonials + App Store star rating
SECURITY STRIP: One-line security badges row + link to Security page
FINAL CTA BLOCK: "Start paying smarter in India" + app store buttons
FOOTER: Full links, compliance text, social, contact

Key Content Copy Guidance:
Headline: Short. Powerful. Non-technical. Not "cross-border fintech UPI bridge" — say: "Your Card Works Everywhere in India Now."
All copy in international English — no Indian colloquialisms. Write like Wise.com or Revolut.
Avoid: "best", "revolutionary", "disrupting" — these signal low credibility to global users.
Show actual screenshots of the app on real phone mockups (not 3D floating devices)

UI Elements Required:
Full-bleed hero with dark navy background + gold accent headline
Animated QR scan mockup (CSS animation, not video — keeps page fast)
Sticky navigation bar with glass-morphism on scroll
App Store + Google Play download buttons (above the fold on mobile)
Logo carousel for trusted partners (Visa / MC / Amex / HDFC / NPCI)

Design Philosophy
"Global trust. Indian soul. Zero clutter."  — The GetB+ design standard is built on three pillars: institutional credibility (Stripe/Wise level), cultural warmth (India-appropriate gold and depth), and radical clarity (every element must earn its place).

Color Palette

Typography System
📌  Use Google Fonts: Inter (primary) or DM Sans (alternative). Both are free, fast-loading, and carry the same institutional credibility as Stripe's Stripe Neue or Wise's Work Sans.

Design Inspiration References

Animation & Motion Direction
"Purposeful, not decorative." — Every animation must serve comprehension, not aesthetics. This is a fintech product. Excessive animation reduces trust.

📌  Tool recommendation: Use Framer Motion (if React/Next.js) or GSAP ScrollTrigger for scroll-based reveals. Lottie for any icon animations. No parallax effects — they cause motion sickness and hurt mobile performance.

Mobile-First Approach
80%+ of GetB+ users will discover the product on mobile — they are either searching from their phone before a trip or seeing it shared in a travel group. Design mobile first. Desktop is the enhanced experience.




CTA Placement Strategy
GetB+'s primary conversion metric is App Downloads. Every CTA on the website should funnel toward that action. Secondary conversion is waitlist signup (for users not ready to download immediately).


📌  Rule: Never have two competing primary CTAs in the same viewport. One action per visible screen area. Secondary CTAs must be visually lighter (ghost button or text link).
